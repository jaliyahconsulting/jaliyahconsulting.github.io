Já·li·yah

Jáliyah uses freedom singing to apply movement values and lessons from the past to change conditions in the contemporary movement.

Our Work

Jaliyah Consulting seeks to connect and support people in recognizing patterns and in challenging and transforming white supremacist culture.

We aim to support commitment to building a more just city, state, and country beyond any borders through:

  • Freedom singing
  • Facilitating story circles
  • Training in story circle process
  • Developing grassroots leadership workshops/trainings
  • Meeting facilitation

Founder

As a cultural worker, activist, facilitator, and educator, Wendi O'Neal works to connect social and economic justice groups' through mission, vision and values with how everyday work gets done. She uses spiritually grounded practices, visual art, story circles and song sharing as tools for growing inspiration and building democratic process.

Born and raised in New Orleans, she has worked in local, regional and national organizations; but her heart's work is rooted in the Deep South of the US, especially the kind of organizing that happens around kitchen tables and good food. She is a dacner, "makers & scrap paper" visual artist, freedom-song-singer and teacher.

One of her favorite teachers is her father, John O'Neal, co-founder of the Free Southern Theater, a Radical Black Theater project that grew out of the Student Non-Violent Coordinating Committee in the 1960's. In 1991, he was one of the founders of the first documented out lesbian and bisexual womens's alliances at a Historically Black College, Spelman, in Atlanta, GA - which continues to exist as the group Afrekete today.
